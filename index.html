The Dangers of becoming a Web Developer

By David Bazashvili, Jenny Raab and Becka Taboada,
10th of October 2022

Three newly-trained web developers share with you a set of warnings to avoid web development as a career choice all together, at all cost. Read carefully and don’t even think about falling into the trap.

Coding is dangerous. You probably think that it is the pathway to a better set of opportunities in the job market with a relatively high income and even to solve some of the world’s biggest problems. This article is intended to warn anyone who might be interested in becoming or already is on their way to become a web developer, that it is never too late to desist from this egregious idea. Learning to program is a long, winding path characterized by the utmost struggle.

Documentation: friend or foe?

Many a coder will assure any newbie that the reason documentation has been invented and continues to be updated is to provide help to solve any coding issue that could arise. In fact, the first rule of WebDev is to look into the documentation. Welp! Good luck not getting lost in it! Trying to find anything in any documentation is a long, torturous process that often times leaves you more confused about a problem than when you started.

Of course, learning to navigate documentation is an exercise only mastered through repetition and perseverance. Well we got news for you: by the time you finally think you mastered any part of it, a new version appears or some part of it gets deprecated or becomes obsolete. Updates and new platforms or technologies are extremely annoying. So it is better not to bother.

Life-long learning: the never-ending doom

Acquiring the skill of coding is hard. Very hard. You have doomed yourself into a life of never-ending learning. You have to constantly master a new language, a new technology, a new version of a library. Your brain will need to undergo constant training to the point that you may become too smart because you have to learn more in one week than what you learned in 10 years of school. You are basically a hamster on a wheel now. Forever.

Learning web development will certainly give you a new perspective on life. Remember how you used to be a cheerful little bugger who believed anything was possible? Anything is indeed possible once you decide to learn to code. Kiss that lovely sense of hope and purpose goodbye.Your entire life will suffer: your professional prospects will change fundamentally, your mental health will be in constant peril, your physical health will deteriorate, and your social life will certainly take a turn.

Web Development: A new Path in life?

As a developer, you need to learn how to debug and how to approach a problem. There is just no way around it. Except nobody will tell you just how annoying debugging or how long the process of solving a problem is. For instance, it is common knowledge that there are many coding challenges that are quite popular, like the renowned Fibonacci Sequence, which is often part of an interview process.

Once you have spent a week trying to solve it without any success, you will lose faith that you will ever be able to unlock that achievement. In fact, you will lose faith that it can even be solved by average minds (like yours). The first of a series of meltdowns/mental breakdowns ensues to the point that you start questioning all of your life choices. This basically entails having unlocked a pathway into a whole new series of existential crises.

But don’t put too much pressure on yourself, because…

Unus pro omnibus, omnes pro uno

Community is the cornerstone of the technologies that we use and develop. As a developer, you will not navigate any of these challenges alone. You will certainly meet new people, and possibly make too many new friends, because you will have no choice but to make friends with other programers. After a while, they will be the only ones to laugh about your jokes because they will be the only ones to understand them. Get ready to buy a whole lot of presents for people outside of your non-existent inner circle, like for Christmas, or Chanukkah, or Eid, or Chinese New Year, or Kwanza, or Day of the Dead, or Diwali, or Summer Solstice, or St. Patrick’s Day, or all of these but not for Valentine’s day.

Communication is Key

A sturdy, loving relationship with your partner or family or dog or cat or plant will turn into a fantasy you no longer care enough to nurture. The thrill of building something new is so intense that you will neglect your non-screen relationships. You will no longer be able to put two coherent sentences together. Speaking like a normal human being to maintain good communication that is grounded in empathy and active listening will be out the window before you can say fibonacci. Indeed, once you really get into coding, you may be able to forget all about your little broken heart, because you will no longer feel anything other than the dopamine hit of making something work or the soothing rage of finding out where that god-forsaken semicolon was missing. If you are a perfectionist, coding will be the most triggering experience of your life.

The mark of a good web developer is perseverance. Giving up is not an option and your resilience will be put to the test on the daily. You may be able to regain some of those communication skills with the myriad of debugging ducks that will probably colonize your entire apartment. Therefore, caring for your physical and mental health must become your top priority. A well rested mind will be able to code better, except for the fact that bugs in your code will decide whether you got to bed at all or even are able to fall asleep. And if you do, guess what you will dream about.

I don’t think you’re ready for this jelly

Your physical health will suffer. You will grow a humpback from hunching over your computer. Your lower back will get so tight that you might want to consider creating diamonds with all that juicy pressure you have single-handedly created. Dehydration is a real threat you will have to grapple with once you start forgetting to take fluids. Your skin will turn a pasty hue due to the lack of sun or real nutrients in your diet, because your eating habits will also change. You will not have the time to invest in feeding yourself properly, so you will either forget to eat or eat just about anything. Kiss that scrumptious little figure of yours goodbye, Sally! You will get either extra fat or extra skinny. Either way, you will become super unattractive and you will die alone, as we already covered that earlier in this article.

Alternatively, you will need someone to feed and care of you. It’s time to rely on what could be left of your inner circle (if there is anyone left in that wasteland). Let yourself be parented by your partner or even your own mother at 34 years old, thus falling into toxic relationship patterns that will lead to nothing but mutual resentment. If you live alone, then eat your cat. Or your plant.

It’s not all bad…

By this point you have noticed the tone of this article may evoke one of despair, but let us assure you that there is great joy in learning how to code your own web application. In fact, you can almost certainly count on joy being the main driver of this new endeavor of yours. As mentioned above, the dopamine hit of solving a problem or debugging your code or making something work is very addictive. Dopamine will become your new drug of choice. It makes you so happy that you forget just how exhausting it was getting there in the first place. Only to hit rock bottom ever so often that you go back for another hit before you know it. Congratulations, you have chosen the path of a junkie.

Coding: a super power

Suddenly, you will feel so powerful that you will start thinking that all the problems of the world could be resolved by coding. Being regarded as a hero (mainly by yourself) will become terribly annoying. Traveling the world will become a real possibility because you can work remotely and your skills are so transferable that you could code from anywhere.

Seriously, stop pursuing web development as a career path. It will mess you up. It will show you all the possibilities of your creativity and problem-solving skills. You don’t want that. And the worst part is that you have no one to blame but your miserable self. You have been thoroughly and fairly warned. Whatever you do, cross web development off of your career choices.

If despite all those warnings, you decide to still go for it, remember:

If it works, don’t touch it. It’s not a bug, it’s a feature. And it’s always working on my machine!
